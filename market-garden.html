Market Gardening

Low-input fruit production · agroecological experimentation · Switzerland

Overview

I operate a small-scale market gardening project focused on low-input fruit production under Swiss climatic conditions. The project combines ecological cultivation principles with systematic observation and evaluation, aiming to explore the feasibility of growing selected subtropical fruit species without energy-intensive infrastructure.

Concept & Experimental Approach

The cultivation system was designed as a mixed planting arrangement, with species and varieties deliberately combined to take advantage of allelopathic interactions and microclimatic effects. Rather than optimizing for maximum yield, the focus was placed on fruit quality, resource efficiency, and long-term system stability.

Cultivation System

All plants were cultivated in containers, enabling the productive use of urban ground otherwise unsuitable for food production. This approach allowed precise control of soil composition and nutrient availability while maintaining flexibility in spatial arrangement.

Water and nutrient inputs were managed using a drip irrigation system, allowing highly efficient and targeted application. No heating systems were used, and no chemical plant protection products were applied during cultivation.

Variety Trials & Evaluation

Across multiple growing seasons, harvest surveys were conducted to assess ripening success, yield stability, and fruit quality of selected subtropical species and varieties under relatively harsh climatic conditions. Particular attention was given to phenological timing, environmental tolerance, and sensory quality at maturity.

These observations were used to identify varieties capable of producing high-quality fruit in Switzerland without the need for energy-intensive interventions.

Outcome & Application

The project demonstrated that selected subtropical fruit varieties can be cultivated successfully under Swiss conditions using low-input methods. Harvested fruits were supplied directly to La Couronne (Solothurn), a fine-dining restaurant recognized by GaultMillau, where quality and consistency were essential criteria.
